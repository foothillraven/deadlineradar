CPE Requirements vs. License Renewal — The Deadline CPAs Mix Up

CPAs juggle two deadlines that sound like they should be the same thing but often aren't: the date your license itself renews, and the date your continuing professional education (CPE) hours are due. Mixing them up is one of the most common ways a CPA ends up scrambling in the last week before a deadline — or worse, finding out their license lapsed because they tracked the wrong date.

They're not always the same date — sometimes they're not even the same frequency.

Take Missouri. The license itself renews every two years, on a fixed September 30 date (Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 20 § 2010-2.070(1)). But CPE hours are checked every single year — 40 hours including 2 ethics hours, due annually, even in the "off year" when the license itself isn't up for renewal (20 CSR 2010-4.010(1)(C)). A Missouri CPA could renew their license this year, relax about paperwork for twelve months, and still owe a full year of CPE hours in that gap year — with a January 1–March 1 grace window to catch up if they fall behind.

West Virginia shows the same pattern a different way: the license itself renews every year on June 30 (W. Va. Code § 30-9-12(a)), but CPE is tracked on a rolling three-year total (120 hours, minimum 20 per year) that must be completed by December 31 each year and reported to the Board by January 31 of the following year (W. Va. CSR tit. 1, ser. 1, § 1-1-7). Renew your license in June and you might assume you're square for the year — but your CPE hours are still due six months later, on a completely different clock.

Not every state splits them this way. Wisconsin, for example, ties CPE directly to the same renewal date — there's no separate CPE-specific cutoff to track (Wis. Stat. § 440.08(2); DSPS Accounting Examining Board). So the honest answer to "are these the same deadline?" is: it depends on your state, and you have to check both rules, not assume.

What happens if you miss one but not the other? The consequences are usually different too — a missed license-renewal deadline typically means your license lapses and you can't practice until you reinstate it (often with a fee). A missed CPE deadline, on the other hand, is often a compliance issue caught at your next renewal, or during an audit of your CPE records — annoying and sometimes costly, but not always an immediate practice-stopping event the way a lapsed license is. Either way, your state board is the authority on what actually happens — this isn't legal advice, just a map of how the two deadlines relate.

The practical fix: don't rely on memory for either one. Find your state's CPA renewal deadline here and set a reminder — and if your state runs CPE on a different clock than your license (like Missouri or West Virginia above), track that separately too.
