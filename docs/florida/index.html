Florida CPA license renewal

Individual CPA license
Date not confirmed

Individual CPA license renewal is a rolling 2-year 'reestablishment period' anchored to each CPA's own original certificate date, not a publicly stated odd/even calendar split. Real active licenses show both odd- and even-year expirations simultaneously (confirmed via DBPR's live license-verification tool), so there is no single date DeadlineRadar can safely publish for every individual visitor.

We previously listed this as two odd/even cohort records, but a 2026-07-05 recovery review found that framing was wrong: individual CPA expiration dates are per-licensee, tied to each person's own original certificate date -- not a public odd/even rule. The codified odd-year rule applies to FIRM licenses only (see Florida's firm page), not individuals. Confirm your exact personal expiration date using the Florida DBPR license-verification lookup (myfloridalicense.com) or on your license.

Firm license
Date not confirmed

Firm licenses expire December 31 of odd-numbered years (a single cohort, simpler than the individual-license split).

We could not confirm the specific 'odd-numbered years' detail against two independent authoritative sources: the Board's own page states firm licenses renew biennially and expire December 31 of odd years, but neither Fla. Stat. 473.3101 nor Florida Administrative Code chapters 61H1-26/61H1-31 state the odd-year parity -- they only confirm a generic 2-year cycle. Confirm your exact renewal date with the Florida Board of Accountancy or on your license. (2026-07-06 update: a full sweep of Fla. Stat. ch. 473 and FAC 61H1-26/61H1-31 in their entirety turned up no additional parity-stating section; Florida HB 607, a 2026 session bill, reportedly proposes extending DBPR renewal cycles to 4 years across professions -- unrelated to closing the current gap, but worth monitoring as a future accuracy risk to this record.)
