New Jersey CPA License Renewal Deadline

New Jersey CPA license renewal

Individual CPA License
Date not confirmed

Triennial (3-year) renewal cycle. The Board's own FAQ (njconsumeraffairs.gov/acc) confirms the triennial structure, that renewal notices are mailed ~60 days before the expiration date printed on the license, and that CPE (120 hours per triennial period, minimum 20/year) is tracked over the same period. It does NOT itself publish the specific current-cycle end date -- that figure (12/31/2026) came only from a trade association (njcpa.org), not the regulator, and per orchestrator review (2026-07-05) does not clear the bar for a published, board-confirmed date. Downgraded from a computed date to a data gap.

NJ renews on a fixed triennial cycle ending December 31; the specific current-cycle end date is not published at the state-board level -- confirm your exact expiration on your license certificate or with the Board directly. (Independently re-checked 2026-07-05 by the orchestrator: the NJ Division of Consumer Affairs board pages confirm the triennial/120-CPE structure but do not publish 2026-12-31 as the current cycle end; that figure traced only to njcpa.org, a trade association.)

CPA Firm Registration/Permit
Date not confirmed

NJ registers accounting firms separately from individuals, and firm registration is also triennial, but whether the firm cycle is synchronized to the same Jan 1-Dec 31 calendar as individual licenses, or runs on a separate offset (e.g. July-June) schedule, could not be confirmed from any official NJ .gov source.

The official Firm Registration Application PDF (njconsumeraffairs.gov) could not be parsed as readable text, and no other official Board page states firm-specific renewal dates. Unverified third-party snippets suggest an offset July-June firm cycle, but this is not confirmed. Do not publish a firm-license reminder date for New Jersey until this is confirmed directly with the Board.
